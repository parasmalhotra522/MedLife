A dry cough that doesn’t produce mucus is a common symptom of a cold which could disrupt your sleep. Besides drinking lots of warm or hot liquids and using a humidifier, ask your pharmacist which cough remedies are right for you, and which can help you get a good night’s sleep. Speak to your pharmacist if your cough doesn’t improve.

If you have a cough that produces mucus, you could have chest congestion, which could also mean you may have a cold. Speak to your pharmacist to ask how products that contain expectorants can help you feel better.

Your pharmacist can help you find fever reducers and pain relievers to manage your symptoms, or you can also check out our featured products.

Your pharmacist can recommend an over-the-counter medication, saline nasal spray, or cool mist humidifier to help you with that annoying sneezing and runny nose. Be sure to talk about your symptoms to find the right products for you.
